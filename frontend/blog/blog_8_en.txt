UN REPORT HIGHLIGHTS AUTOMATED PAYOUTS PROTECTING FARMERS FROM SEVERE DROUGHTS
Date: July 3, 2026

According to an annual report on UN-backed agricultural programs, the adoption of parametric insurance in developing economies has increased by 40% year-on-year.

Why Parametric?
Unlike traditional indemnity insurance, parametric models require no physical loss adjusters to evaluate claims. Payouts are triggered automatically using satellite data or meteorological records:
- For example, if precipitation falls below a threshold (drought index), payouts are credited to farmers' accounts within days.
- This allows agricultural workers to maintain liquidity ahead of the next planting cycle.

Actuaries in this segment focus on defining optimal triggers to minimize basis risk for policyholders.
