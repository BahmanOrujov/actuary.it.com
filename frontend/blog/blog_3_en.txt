CENTRAL BANK OF AZERBAIJAN INTRODUCES STRICTER CERTIFICATION AND IFRS 17 COMPLIANCE FOR LOCAL ACTUARIES
Date: July 3, 2026

The Central Bank of Azerbaijan (CBA) has approved new regulatory rules to govern actuarial activities and strengthen oversight in the domestic insurance market.

Key Policy Directives:
1. IFRS 17 Alignment: Local insurance companies must align their reserve valuations and financial reports submitted to the CBA fully with IFRS 17. The discount rates used must follow CBA-approved methodologies.
2. Examination & Certification: Appointed actuaries in domestic firms must now complete at least the foundational exams of recognized global actuarial bodies (such as IFoA, SOA, or equivalents).
3. Reserving Adequacy Reports: Insurers must submit an independent actuarial opinion verifying the adequacy of their technical reserves annually.

The CBA stated that the reforms aim to increase transparency, protect policyholders, and proactively prevent financial distress in the local insurance sector.
