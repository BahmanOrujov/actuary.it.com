CLIMATE LOSSES DRIVE GLOBAL REINSURERS TO RAISE CATASTROPHE COVER RATES BY 15-20%
Date: July 3, 2026

Following extreme weather events and record flood and storm losses in the first half of 2026, global reinsurers have sharply increased their pricing at renewals.

Drivers and Impacts:
- Catastrophic Insurance Losses: Natural disasters across the US and Europe caused billions in insured losses.
- Rate Hikes: Leading reinsurers including Swiss Re, Munich Re, and Hannover Re have increased catastrophe cover rates by an average of 15% to 20%.
- Domestic Spillover: Higher reinsurance costs inflate domestic insurers' operating expenses, which is expected to translate into higher property and motor premiums for retail consumers.

Actuaries at primary insurers are actively restructuring retention limits to optimize reinsurance expenditures.
