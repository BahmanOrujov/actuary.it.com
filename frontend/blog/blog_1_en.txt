INTERNATIONAL ACTUARIAL ASSOCIATION (IAA) PUBLISHES GLOBAL STANDARDS ON AI ADOPTION IN ACTUARIAL WORK
Date: July 3, 2026

The International Actuarial Association (IAA) has released its first official global standards regarding the use of Generative AI and Large Language Models (LLMs) in insurance pricing and actuarial reserving.

Key Regulatory Principles:
1. Explainability: AI models utilized by actuaries must not operate as 'black boxes'. The underlying mathematical logic behind their outputs must be fully transparent and explainable.
2. Data Privacy: Individual and medical client data must be processed only within secure cloud environments or local (on-premise) servers. Sharing this data with public LLM APIs is strictly prohibited.
3. Human-in-the-Loop: No tariff or reserve output generated by AI can be implemented without formal review, validation, and sign-off by a qualified actuary.

The President of the IAA stated that these guidelines aim to mitigate systemic risks associated with rapid AI adoption while safeguarding insurers' financial solvency. Local regulatory bodies are expected to incorporate these standards into national frameworks over the coming months.
